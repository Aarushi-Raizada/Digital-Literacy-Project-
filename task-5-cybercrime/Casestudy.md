## Case Study: UPI Fraud

Rohit, a college student, decided to sell his old phone on an online marketplace to earn some extra money. After posting the ad, he quickly got a message from a person who seemed genuinely interested. The buyer agreed to pay immediately through UPI and sounded polite and convincing.

Soon, the buyer sent Rohit a QR code and told him to scan it to receive the payment. Rohit didn’t think much about it and trusted the process. However, as soon as he scanned the code, instead of receiving money, ₹15,000 was deducted from his bank account. Shocked and confused, he tried calling the buyer, but the number was already switched off.

This kind of fraud is quite common. Scammers usually pretend to be genuine buyers or sellers, gain the victim’s trust, and then create urgency. They send QR codes or payment requests, tricking people into approving transactions without realizing they are actually sending money.

Students, elderly people, and those who are not very familiar with digital payments are easy targets. The result is not just financial loss but also stress and a feeling of being cheated.

This incident shows how important it is to stay alert online. One should always remember that receiving money through UPI does not require scanning a QR code sent by strangers.
